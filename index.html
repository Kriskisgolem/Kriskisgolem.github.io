Races

Humanlike Races

Human
Description: Humans are told to have been the dominant race on Nirva before the Second Conjunction, but the cataclysm decimated their numbers. Most of the conquering races that came inhabited the same climates as humans and halflings, preventing them from reforming any nation of their own. Since then, humans are a minority, living anywhere they can. Most races fear them and are glad for this fact, because humans are generally healthier and better at everything. They are naturally skillful and each of them possesses an inborn talent.
Humans are humanoids with round ears. They have unique skin tones, shared only by other Humanlike races. Humans are known for their varied looks.

Creature Type(s): Humanoid, Humanborn
Ability Score Increase: Gain +2 to one score and +1 to all other ability scores.
Age: You reach adulthood at the age of 16, you start growing old at the age of 50, you are very old starting from the age of 70 and can live up to around 100 years.
Size: Your size is medium (Height range 150cm-210cm).
Great Vitality: Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
Skillful: You have proficiency in two skills of your choice.
Inborn Talent: You gain expertise in an already owned skill (expertise doubles your proficiency bonus for the chosen skill).
Languages: Common and 'the language of the nation you were born in' (generally, but you can choose others based on your origin).

Goliath
Description: Goliaths had a small population even before the Second Conjunction. Even though their number diminished in the cataclysm, none of the conquering races were acclimate to altitudes as high as goliaths lived. This means they remained the only inhabitants of their snowy mountain peaks. Goliaths traditionally live in tribes and regularly climb down to demand tribute from nearby settlements and raiding them if they weren't given their due.
Goliaths are humanoids with round ears, with a tendency of being more muscular and vigorous. They have pale, greyish skin tones and like to wear tribal tattoos. Goliaths are on the smaller side of the Large size category.

Creature Type(s): Humanoid, Humanborn
Ability Score Increase: Gain +2 to one score and +1 to another or +1 to three scores.
Age: You reach adulthood at the age of 16, you start growing old at the age of 50, you are very old starting from the age of 65 and can live up to around 90 years.
Size: Your size is large (Height range 245cm-270cm). Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level. You must eat 2 rations/day.
Giant Build: Your base walking speed is 35 feet. You count as Large in Shove/Grapple checks, but you still cannot Grapple a Huge or larger creature.
Natural Athlete: You have proficiency in the Athletics skill.
Great Vitality: Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
Mountain Born: You have resistance to cold damage. You also naturally acclimate to high altitudes, even if you've never been to one. This includes elevations above 20,000 feet.
Immovable: You have advantage on ability checks and saving throws against forced movement (this includes spells like Thunderwave and Telekinezis).
Languages: Common and Humanic (generally, but you can choose others based on your origin).

Halfling
Description: Not much is known about the Halflings' role in societies before the Second Conjunction, but the cataclysm left them on the brink of extinction as well. Most of the conquering races that came inhabited the same climates as humans and halflings, preventing them from reforming any nation of their own. Since then, halflings are a minority, living anywhere they can. They are usually disliked by societies, because their evolutionary talents make them adept burglars and pickpockets. Altough nature most likely made them so they can elude larger creatures, halflings can outstandingly utilize their feature in modern warfare.
Halflings are short humanoids with round ears. They have unique skin tones, shared only by other Humanlike races. They are a Small race, but they have the Humanlike tendency of being generally more muscular.

Creature Type(s): Humanoid, Humanborn
Ability Score Increase: Gain +2 to one score and +1 to another or +1 to three scores.
Age: You reach adulthood at the age of 18, you start growing old at the age of 100, you are very old starting from the age of 140 and can live up to around 200 years.
Size: Your size is small (Height range 100cm-130cm). Your hit point maximum decreases by 1, and it decreases by 1 every time you gain a level. You only eat 0.5 rations/day.
Small Legs: Your base walking speed is 25 feet.
Great Vitality: Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
Nimble: You can move through the space of any creature that is of a size larger than yours. Opportunity attacks are made against you at a disadvantage.
Elusive: You gain advantage when a creature with a larger size than yours tries to Shove/Grapple you.
Trickfoot: You gain a +2 bonus to your AC unless you're Blinded, Restrained or Incapacitated.
Escape Instinct: You have advantage on initiative rolls. During your first turn in combat, you gain the effects of a Dash and a Disengage (this always applies to the first turn in which you can act). When the combat ends, you cannot gain these first round bonuses again until 1 minute has passed.
Languages: Common and 'the language of the nation you were born in' (generally, but you can choose others based on your origin).

Dwarf
Description: Dwarves were a numerous race on Nirva before the Second Conjunction, but the cataclysm decimated their numbers. Most of the conquering races that came were surface dwellers, allowing the dwarves to recover relatively well. Their main rivals are the goblins, who know populate a large portion of old dwarven territories. Dwarves are a proud and hardy race who make their home in mountains, where they mostly build underground. Their Stone Sense, darkvision and resilience helps them navigate the caves, while their Ironblood gives them an innate talent in craftsmanship. Dwarven kingdoms are famous for their mining, masonry and forging expertise.
Dwarves are humanoids with round ears. They have unique skin tones, shared only by other Humanlike races. Dwarves are short and have small legs compared to the rest of their body. They mostly possess stocky builds with large arms. Their hands are generally larger and outsize the hands of other medium humanoids most of the time, altough this is hardly ever true for dwarf females. Male Dwarves made a strong tradition out of growing beards.

Creature Type(s): Humanoid, Humanborn
Ability Score Increase: Gain +2 to one score and +1 to another or +1 to three scores.
Age: You reach adulthood at the age of 18, you start growing old at the age of 75, you are very old starting from the age of 105 and can live up to around 150 years.
Size: Your size is medium (Height range 140cm-160cm).
Small Legs: Your base walking speed is 25 feet.
Darkvision: Accustomed to life underground, you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions. You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
Great Vitality: Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
Dwarven Resilience: You have advantage on saving throws against poison, and you have resistance against poison damage.
Dwarven Toughness: Thanks to your tough skin and bone structure, whenever you take piercing, slashing or bludgeoning damage you reduce it by 1. Whenever you take acid, fire, cold, lightning or thunder damage you reduce it by 3.
Ironblood: You gain proficiency with one of the following tools: Mason's Tools, Jeweler's Tools, Smith's Tools. This proficiency doesn't count against your tool limit.
Stone Sense: Granted by your racial magic you can easily read informations from stone surfaces, and always know which way is North. The readable informations are the stone's type, it's "depth" (you'll know if there's space behind it in close proximity) and any marks left by creatures (even ones that cannot be seen with normal vision).
Languages: Common and Humanic (generally, but you can choose others based on your origin).

Duergar
Description: People belive that during the old times, dwarves went to live so deep under the earth, that they became Kranic. What we know for sure is that Duergar lived in the Underdark Tunnels, even before the Second Conjunction. They too felt the effects of the cataclysm, which made them unable to stop the elves from making a foothold in their homeland. Even from a disadvantageous position, they managed to retreat and replenish enough to take up fight against the invaders, who later became known as Drows. Because ark doesn't reach the Underdark, Kranic creatures developed and lived without it. While they cannot use magic, they're immune to magic itself. Like all Kranics who come to the surface, Duergars are treated with fear and disgust, which pushes them towards crime. We know that Duergar society is godless, feudal, patriarchal and that they're still waging guerilla warfare against the Drow but little else.
Duergars are humanoids with round ears. They have white skin, which changes to blue/purple once they approach the surface. Altough they are always born with white hair and red iris, this can also change along with their skin color in rare cases. Duergars are short and have small legs compared to the rest of their body. They mostly possess stocky builds with large arms. Their hands are generally larger and outsize the hands of other medium humanoids most of the time, altough this is hardly ever true for Duergar females. Male Duergars made a strong tradition out of growing beards.

Creature Type(s): Humanoid, Kranic
Ability Score Increase: Gain +2 to one score and +1 to another or +1 to three scores.
Age: You reach adulthood at the age of 18, but you don't grow old, which means your body stays in its prime. If you ever leave the Underdark, your body will deteriorate at the cellular level and begin aging. You start growing old after 50 years, you are very old starting from 70 years and can live up to around 100 years.
Size: Your size is medium (Height range 140cm-160cm).
Small Legs: Your base walking speed is 25 feet.
Dwarven Resilience: You have advantage on saving throws against poison, and you have resistance against poison damage.
Superior Darkvision: Accustomed to the Underdark Tunnels, you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions. You can see in dim light within 120 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
Sunlight Hypersensitivity: While standing in sunlight, you have disadvantage on all ability checks, attack rolls and saving throws and receive vulnerability to all damage. Clothes cannot protect you from this, only the shadows cast by especially solid structures. You can only see out to a range of 120ft through sunlight.
Spellcasting Inability: You can't cast spells. You can only attune to a very limited amount of magic items. Your body is compatible with chrome, but you aren't able to use chrome prosthetics (meaning that you can effectively replace missing bones with chrome, but you won't be able to move or see through a chrome eye that you placed in).
Works (doesn't affect body, doesn't cast magic): Gloves of Thievery, Boots of Elvenkind, Armor of Resistance, Bracers of Defence, *damaging weapons*
Doesn't work: Stone of Good Luck, Cloak of Protection, Eyes of Eagle, ...almost every magic item
Magic Immunity: Your body is immune to all magical effects and damage and can't be targeted by magical effects. (Both hostile and friendly, like Cure Wounds. You can't see through magical darkness, but you can move through magical barriers, altough none of your items or clothes can.
Conjuration spells like Entangle can't target you with their effect, but it's area is still difficult terrain for you and the fog summoned by Fog Cloud limits your vision normally, while Hunger of Hadar has no effect on you.
Illusion spells like Disguise Self and Invisibility project a general image, which means you see them like others do. But other illusion spells like Phantasmal Force still can't target you or hurt you in any way.)
You cannot be teleported in any way, which means you're forever bound to Nirva.
Languages: Common and Durgish (generally, but you can choose others based on your origin).

Elven Races

High Elf
Description: High Elves are descendants of the Eladrin Elves, who came from Feyvi during the Second Conjunction. The Eladrin formed four large groups who settled differently on their new home, Nirva. Due to the significant differences in the atmospheres, the Eladrin changed after their migration. (Fey creatures are volatile and cannot preserve their original nature outside Feyvi.) The High Elves, who settled as surface city dwellers, became the most widespread. They possess an unmatched affinity for spellcasting. While most "magical" races gain the same powers, High Elves develop the ability to use a wide range of them.
High Elves are pointy-eared humanoids with yellow/golden skin tones. Their skins can manifest natural, magic infused markings. Their height is generally tall.

Creature Type(s): Humanoid, Humanborn
Ability Score Increase: Gain +2 to one score and +1 to another or +1 to three scores.
Age: You reach adulthood at the age of 20, you start growing old at the age of 150, you are very old starting from the age of 210 and can live up to around 300 years.
Size: Your size is medium (Height range 160cm-210cm).
Fey Ancestry: You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep.
Trance: Elves do not sleep. Instead they meditate deeply, remaining semi-conscious, for 7 hours a day. The common word for this meditation is "trance".
Arcane Aptitude: You have expertise in the Arcana skill.
High Elven Magic: You learn one cantrip of your choice from the Sorcerer spell list. Later, at level three, you learn one 1st level sorcerer spell and cast it once/long rest. At level five you can learn one 2nd level sorcerer spell and cast it once/long rest.
Magical Flexibility: You may change your choosen spells/cantrip from the High Elven Magic feature whenever you finish a long rest.
Languages: Common and Elven (generally, but you can choose others based on your origin).

Rock Gnomes are short, pointy-eared humanoids, with skin tones similiar to "white Humans". Altough Halflings are a bit taller, have a wider range of skin tones and are generally more muscular, Rock Gnomes are commonly confused with them if their ear isn't showing.

They have heightened reflexes, supreme agility and an outstanding nightvision. Tabaxi are deft predators, feared by many across Nirva.
Tabaxi are feline humanoids with furry skin. They have cat-like heads and tails, and come in various shapes, sizes and colors. They possess sharp teeth and even sharper claws.

Creature Type(s): Humanoid, Beast
Ability Score Increase: Gain +2 to one score and +1 to another or +1 to three scores.
Age: You reach adulthood at the age of 8, you start growing old at the age of 50, you are very old starting from the age of 65 and can live up to around 80 years.
Size: Your size is medium (Height range 150cm-190cm).
Superior Darkvision: Accustomed to hunting in the night, you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions. You can see in dim light within 120 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
Feline Agility: You have proficiency in the Acrobatics skill. While you aren't Blinded/Restrained/Incapacitated you doesn't receive damage/fall prone when falling from 10ft and cannot land on your head (from any height).
Susceptible Fur: Burning progresses 2x faster for you.
Raw Meat Eating: You can easily digest and gain nutrition from raw meat.
Beastial Nature: You have proficiency in Perception and Animal Handling.
Sharp Claws: Your claws are natural weapons you're proficient with. They count as 'finesse' and deal 1d6 + modifier slashing damage.
Bite: You can bite enemies as an attack. It counts as a natural weapon you're proficient with. You gain disadvantage on your attack if your target isn't Incapacitated/Restrained/Blinded, unless you're in grappling contact with them which grants you advantage on your attack roll. Your bite deals 1d6 + Strength modifier piercing damage.
Heightened Reflexes: You gain a +5 bonus to your initiative. You can take an additional reaction, but you cannot use this again until the start of your next turn. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest. (you can only cast one leveled spell as a reaction between your turns)
Four-Legged Run: When you move on your turn, you can double your speed until the end of the turn. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you move 0 feet on one of your turns.
Languages: Common and Tabaxi (generally, but you can choose others based on your origin).

Draconic Races

Dragonborn
Description: Dragonborns came from Drakaris, along with Kobolds and Dragons during the Second Conjunction. They were part of the Dragon Empire until it was destroyed by an alliance between the Celestial Gods. Since then, most Dragonborns are living as a minority in foreign kingdoms. They possess the same magical traits as most Draconic creatures, having blindsight, resistant scales and breath weapon.
Dragonborns are tall, scaly humanoids with claws and dragon-like heads. Their scale color depends on their element. They may or may not have tails.

Creature Type(s): Humanoid, Draconic
Ability Score Increase: Gain +2 to one score and +1 to another or +1 to three scores.
Age: You reach adulthood at the age of 15, you start growing old at the age of 200, you are very old starting from the age of 280 and can live up to around 400 years.
Size: Your size is medium (Height range 170cm-210cm).
Chromatic Element: Your color is ascociated with a certain element. (Black/Green = Acid / Blue/Yellow = Lightning / Red/Orange = Fire / White/Beige = Cold).
Draconic Scales: You gain resistance to your element. You also reduce all incoming damage by 1, except for poison, necrotic, radiant and psychic damage.
Draconic Sense: Your racial magic grants you blindsight within 30ft.
Claws: Your claws are natural weapons you're proficient with. They count as 'finesse' and deal 1d4 + modifier slashing damage.
Elemental Bite: You can bite enemies as an attack. It counts as a natural weapon you're proficient with. You gain disadvantage on your attack if your target isn't Paralyzed/Unconscious, unless you're in grappling contact with them which grants you advantage on your attack roll. Your bite damage equals 1d6 + Strength modifier piercing damage and 1d4 of your element.
Breath Weapon: Starting from level three, once/turn as an attack, or as a bonus action if your action was Attack or Cast cantrip/spell this turn, you can magically exhale your element in either a "15' cone" or a "5' by 30' line", you choose. Each creature in the area makes a Dexterity saving throw to halve the damage. The damage is 'prof.bonus'd10+'prof.bonus' of your element. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus and regain the uses of it when you finish a long rest.
Languages: Common and 'the language of the nation you were born in' (generally, but you can choose others based on your origin).

Tieflings

Spawn of Kandros
Description: Children offered to Kandros' Infernal Legion, before they were born, become Spawns of Kandros. They're rarely seen outside the Legion's ranks. Spawns outside the Legion were either not delivered in the end, deserted later on, or failed their training during childhood. Children who fail the training (without dying) are left in the area they were born in. Spawns of Kandros are more likely to be strong, dexterous and disciplined, making them natural born warriors.
Spawns of Kandros mostly resemble an individual of their Heritage Race. They have the same ear, they're around the same height and build. Their skin tone is either one from their Heritage Race or red/yellow. They have horns and may develop other devil features as well, like tail, claws and sharp teeth.

Creature Type(s): Humanoid, Fiend
Ability Score Increase: Gain +2 to one score and +1 to another or +1 to three scores.
Heritage: You have a Heritage Race which must be a Humanoid that doesn't have the Beast/Draconic/Elemental type. Your reproductive organs are unaffected by your hellish features.
Age: You age according to your Heritage Race.
Size: You have the same size as your Heritage Race (Height range is the same as your Heritage Races'). Your size affects your HP but not your speed.
Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
Great Vitality: Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
Unyielding: You're immune to being Frightened.
Flames of Magna: You have resistance to fire damage.
Hellfire: You learn the Fire Bolt cantrip.
Fiendish Vigor: Starting from level three, you can cast False Life on yourself at will. You also learn False Life as a spell and whenever you cast False Life (freely or with a spell slot), you gain a bonus to the temporary hit points gained equal to half your level (rounded up). When casting False Life, there are no components for it. (like it's a Subtle Spell)
Languages: Common and 'the language of the nation you were born in' (generally, but you can choose others based on your origin).
